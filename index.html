I am a Software Developer from Northeastern Pennsylvania.

As a child I always loved learning how to use my parents computer. Figuring out how to solve a problem or create something new always amazed me. I enjoyed the challenge of learning ways to use computers and software. Years later, this passion lead me to seek out secondary education

College

I started my secondary education journey at Luzerne County Community College. There, I learned what I really wanted to do. I wanted to be a software developer. In my time there, I became vice president of the Computer Information Systems Club, maintained the dean's list, and learned a lot of key skills that would help me throughout my career. I received my associate's degree in Computer Information Systems in May of 2013.

Before graduating from my associate's degree program, I knew I wanted to further my education. I moved on to Penn State University to continue learning. There I maintained dean's list while learning even more vital skills to set me forth on my career journey. I graduated with a Bachelor's Degree in Information Sciences and Technologies in December of 2015

Career

A few months prior to graduating Penn State, I started an internship with TMG Health as a Production Coordinator. I was responsible for creating automated claims processing processes, as well as automation of reports. This was my first foray into the world of development as a career. After probation, I was hired on full time as I continued to learn and develop my skillset for programming.

I found an opportunity to do Web Development at the United States Postal Service after some time passed at TMG. I was hired as an associate analyst/programmer in 2016. Shortly after that I was promoted to mid-level within my first year. I continued my hunt for a stronger skill set and suring up weaknesses in my career since then. I've worked on a customer service and administrative application since then coming up from the bottom to being in charge of the whole application. More recently I was promoted to a Senior level programmer and have done a fair bit of training new people, creating new pieces of functionality, and learning.

Goals

Throughout my journey in Software Development I have maintained a few key goals:

  • To continue to learn and develop skills that will further my abilities in software development and beyond
  • To find new and innovative ways to accomplish tasks
  • To help design and create something bigger than me
